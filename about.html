Definition of drugs and alcohol abuse

Drug : Drug is any substances used as medication or in the preparation of medication.

Abuse: Abuse is the inappropriate use of any substances,especially those that alter conciousness(e.g alcohol,cocaine etc.)

Alcohol: Alcohol is a fermented liquid made up of distilled hydrocarbons that's also known as ethanol.

Drug and Alcohol abuse : Drug and alcohol abuse is the habitual taking of addictive or illegal drugs or the wrong use of drugs and other substances.

Factors Contributing to Drugs And Alchol Abuse
1. Biological Factors
  1. Family history.
  2. Reinforcing effect of drug.
  3. Withdrawal effect and craving.
2. Psychological Factors
  1. Curiosity.
  2. Social rebelliousness.
  3. Sensation seeking (feeling high).
  4. Poor control.
  5. Low-self esteem (anomie).
3. Social Factors
  1. Peer Pressure.
  2. Role Model.
  3. Conflicts.
  4. Religious Reasons.
  5. Social Attitude.

Causes of drugs and Alcohol abuse
  1. Poverty: This is the state of being inferior or insufficient in amount.
  2. Bad company: This is the act of spending time with people who are not morally good.
  3. Peer pressure: This is the feelings that one must do the same thing as other people of one's age and social group in order to be liked or respected by them.
  4. Family problem: This is when conflict between related family members becomes ewspecially intense or frequents,to the points that it interrupts your day-to-day life/activities.
  5. Financial issues: The term financial issues and financial strain typically used to describe a difficult or challenging financial situation.

Drugs Commomly misued
  1. Codeine and Morphine: These drugs dull pain, but in large doses they can also cause a euphoric high -- and dangerous side effects.
  2. Amphetamines: High doses can cause a dangerous rise in body temperature, irregular heartbeat, and even cardiac arrest.
  3. Marijuana: t can stimulate the nervous system enough that some people become paranoid while taking it.‚Äù At the same time, marijuana can dull memory
  4. Cocaine: Cocaine tightens blood vessels and speeds up the heart. These cardiovascular effects are the main reason for most cocaine-related deaths.
  5. Methamphetamine: Can speed up the heart, as well as cause hyperthermia, an extremely high body temperature. When used over a long period of time, methamphetamine can cause anxiety, insomnia, and even psychotic symptoms, like hallucinations.
  6. Heroin: Heroin is very addictive. Many people who take it develop a use disorder. This means it causes health problems, disabilities, and trouble at home, work, or school.
